German Rucksack Model 1931 (M31) — one of the main field packs used by the Wehrmacht and Elite in WWII

Officially introduced in 1931 as part of the reorganization of infantry field gear. It was meant to replace the heavy Tornister (“Affe” monkey pack), especially for troops not carrying the full marching pack (Marschgepäck). The chamges in M31 model was introduced in 1939 year and adjasted to use with Y-Straps. Used widely by infantry, artillery, and support troops, while mountain troops (Gebirgsjäger) had their own larger rucksack design.

This example issued probably in Medium wartime period. This identified by specific hooks type and general construction-materials details
