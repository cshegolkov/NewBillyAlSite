German Tornister, Model 1934 (M34 Tornister) — one of the standard field packs of the early Wehrmacht. Introduced in 1934 as an improved version of the WWI Tornister, it featured a square body made of field-grey canvas, a rigid frame, and a cowhide-covered back panel. The flap was closed with two vertical leather straps, and the interior offered a single main compartment with a small pocket for documents or personal items.

The M34 could be carried with its own straps or attached to the Y-straps, by replacing straps to short M39 version, together with other equipment. Compared to the later M39 Tornister, it was more compact and made with higher-quality leather and stitching. Although gradually replaced by the M39, the M34 continued to be issued throughout the war.

This example is in perfect condition, has matched shoulder straps, which is pretty rare.
